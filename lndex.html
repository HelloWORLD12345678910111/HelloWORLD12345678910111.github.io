Overview

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world, covering most of North Africa. It is known for its vast stretches of sand dunes, extreme temperatures, and unique ecosystem.

Geography

The Sahara spans across several countries, including Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, Egypt, Mali, Niger, Chad, Sudan, and Mauritania. It covers approximately 9.2 million square kilometers.

Climate

The Sahara experiences an arid desert climate with scorching temperatures during the day and cold nights. Rainfall is minimal, and the desert is characterized by its dry, barren landscapes.

Flora and Fauna

Despite its harsh conditions, the Sahara is home to various plant and animal species adapted to desert life. These include camels, desert foxes, and unique desert plants.

History

The Sahara has a rich history, with evidence of ancient civilizations, trade routes, and archaeological discoveries. It played a significant role in the movement of people and goods in ancient times.
